La Siguanaba

La Siguanaba is a spectral woman from Salvadoran folklore who appears at night near rivers or lonely paths. She is said to lure men with her beauty, often appearing as a stunning young woman from afar. However, when they approach, her face transforms into something horrifying— sometimes described as a skull, a horse's face, or a monstrous visage.

According to legend, she was once a woman cursed by the gods for her deceitful and immoral behavior, condemned to wander eternally and frighten those who fall for her illusion. Her story serves both as a cautionary tale about temptation and as a chilling reminder of the supernatural presence believed to haunt the Salvadoran countryside.
